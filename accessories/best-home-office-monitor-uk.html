What to Look for in a Home Office Monitor

Resolution: QHD vs FHD at 27 Inches

At 27 inches, the difference between QHD (2560×1440) and FHD (1920×1080) is immediately visible when reading text. QHD delivers 109 pixels per inch; FHD delivers 82. That difference translates to sharper characters, less font aliasing, and meaningfully less eye fatigue after a full working day. If you're buying a 27-inch monitor for office work, QHD is worth the price premium — which has fallen significantly in the last two years.

USB-C: The Single-Cable Setup

A monitor with USB-C Power Delivery lets you connect your laptop with one cable that handles video signal, data, and laptop charging simultaneously. This means no separate charger on your desk, no cable management headache, and instant dock/undock in one plug. Look for at least 65W Power Delivery — sufficient for most laptops. MacBook Pro 14" users should look for 90W+ if they want to charge at full speed.

Stand Adjustability

A monitor you can adjust properly is one you'll actually have positioned correctly. Height adjustment is the most important — your eye line should be at or slightly below the top of the screen. Tilt adjustment lets you angle the panel to reduce reflections. Swivel and pivot (rotating to portrait orientation) are useful but not essential for most home workers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best home office monitor UK in 2026?
The Dell S2725DS is the best home office monitor UK for most people in 2026. It combines a 27-inch QHD IPS panel, fully adjustable stand, USB-C input, and built-in speakers at around £279 on Amazon.co.uk. The Philips 27E1N1600AE delivers very similar performance at £199 if budget is a priority.
Is QHD worth it for a home office monitor?
Yes, at 27 inches. QHD (2560×1440) delivers noticeably sharper text than FHD (1920×1080) at this screen size — the difference is most apparent when reading documents, spreadsheets, and code for long periods. The eye fatigue reduction is real. For displays below 24 inches, FHD is generally sufficient.
Do I need USB-C on a home office monitor?
If your laptop has a USB-C port, it's genuinely useful. One cable connects your laptop to the monitor, charges it, and passes data — no separate charger, no extra cables. If you frequently move between your desk and other locations, this one-cable setup makes a practical difference every day. Look for at least 65W Power Delivery for most laptops.
What size monitor is best for home office use?
27 inches is the sweet spot for most home office setups. It provides enough screen real estate for two documents side by side without dominating the desk. For deep desks (60cm+), 32 inches works well. For desks under 60cm deep, 24 inches keeps the monitor at a comfortable viewing distance and reduces the need to move your head side to side.
