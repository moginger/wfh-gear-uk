What to Look for in an Ergonomic Chair Under £200

Most of the marketing language around budget ergonomic chairs is noise. The four features that actually matter are:

1. Adjustable Lumbar Support

Fixed lumbar supports — those moulded plastic curves — don't fit most spines. You need height adjustment at minimum; depth adjustment (forward/backward) as well if you can get it. The Sihoo M18 offers both, which is why it wins at this price point. Without adjustable lumbar support, a chair will cause back discomfort for most people within two to three hours of sitting.

2. Breathable Mesh Back

The UK climate is mild, but a full working day in a foam-backed chair creates noticeable heat build-up. Mesh backs allow air to circulate continuously. Any chair under £200 that doesn't have a mesh back is a compromise — the breathability difference is tangible within a couple of hours of sitting.

3. Gas-Lift Height Adjustment

This is table stakes — every chair in this roundup has it. The key number is the adjustment range. Your feet should sit flat on the floor with your knees at a 90-degree angle. If you're tall (over 6ft) or shorter (under 5'4"), check the adjustment range before buying.

4. Adjustable Armrests

Basic height-adjustable arms (2D) are fine for most people. If you type for long periods, 3D arms — which also pivot and move forward/backward — make a real difference to shoulder and wrist positioning. The Sihoo M57 and Boulies EP200 both offer 3D arms under £230.

What features should I look for in an ergonomic chair UK?
The four features that matter most are: adjustable lumbar support (height and depth), a breathable mesh backrest, gas-lift height adjustment, and adjustable armrests. A chair with all four covers the majority of ergonomic needs. Headrests are less important than most marketing suggests — most people don't use them correctly.
