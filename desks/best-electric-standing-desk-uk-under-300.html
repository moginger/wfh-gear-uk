What to Look for in an Electric Standing Desk Under £300

1. Single Motor vs Dual Motor — This Matters More Than Price

The single most important spec to understand when buying an electric standing desk is the motor configuration. Single-motor desks drive the lift from one side, which creates uneven lifting under load and introduces wobble. They're also louder — typically 55–65dB — which is noticeable if you adjust height during calls. Dual-motor desks drive each leg independently, resulting in smoother, faster, and quieter adjustment. The FlexiSpot E7 and E6 Max are dual-motor; the E1 Plus and Fezibo are single-motor.

2. Load Capacity — More Important If You Use Dual Monitors

A single monitor, laptop, keyboard and mouse typically weighs 15–25kg total. Budget desks rated to 70kg have more than enough capacity for this setup. If you run dual monitors or large ultrawide screens, a 100–125kg rated desk gives better stability and avoids putting stress on the motor over time.

3. Height Range — Check Your Numbers Before Buying

The correct standing desk height for you depends on your height. At 5'8", you'll typically want to stand at around 100–105cm. At 6'2", closer to 115cm. Check the maximum height of any desk you're considering — some budget options top out at 118cm, which is too low for taller users. The FlexiSpot E7 reaches 123cm; the E1 Plus reaches 121cm.

4. Anti-Collision Detection

If you lower your desk and something is in the way — a chair, a bag, a pet — a desk with anti-collision detection will stop automatically. Without it, the motor keeps pushing, which can damage both the desk and whatever it hits. All FlexiSpot models in this guide include this feature.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best electric standing desk UK under £300?

Are electric standing desks worth it for home offices?
Yes, for anyone who sits 6+ hours daily. Research consistently shows that alternating between sitting and standing reduces lower back pain, improves afternoon energy levels, and increases daily step count. The health benefit compounds over years of use. Most users who switch to a standing desk report they would not return to a fixed-height desk.
What height should my standing desk be set to?
For standing, your desk should be at elbow height — when you stand naturally, your elbows should be at roughly 90 degrees when resting on the desk surface. For most people this is between 95cm and 115cm. For sitting, the same 90-degree elbow rule applies, typically between 70cm and 80cm. Save both positions as memory presets so switching takes one button press.
How noisy are electric standing desks?
Quality dual-motor electric standing desks operate between 40–50dB during adjustment — roughly equivalent to a quiet conversation. Budget single-motor desks typically run at 55–65dB and adjust more slowly. If you adjust height during video calls, a dual-motor desk is noticeably less disruptive. The FlexiSpot E7 adjusts in around 3–4 seconds for a full range of motion.
